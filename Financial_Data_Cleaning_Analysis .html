# --- STEP 1: CLEAN CURRENCY NAMES ---
currency_pattern = r'(\$|INR|USD|EUR|GBP|JPY|₹|£|€)'
extracted_currency = df['Amount'].astype(str).str.extract(currency_pattern, flags=re.IGNORECASE, expand=False)
df['Currency'] = df['Currency'].fillna(extracted_currency).fillna('Unknown').str.upper()

# --- STEP 2: CONVERT TO NUMERIC ---
# 1. Handle parentheses like (100.00) -> -100.00
df['Amount'] = df['Amount'].astype(str).str.replace(r'^\((.*)\)$', r'-\1', regex=True)
# 2. Keep only numbers, dots, and minus signs
df['Amount'] = df['Amount'].str.replace(r'[^\d\.-]', '', regex=True)
# 3. Convert to float and fill empty/text ("free") with 0.0
df['Amount'] = pd.to_numeric(df['Amount'], errors='coerce').fillna(0.0)

# --- STEP 3: FIX THE SIGNS (The "Force" Logic) ---
# We make everything positive first, then decide the sign based on Category.
# This ensures "Dining" is ALWAYS negative, even if the CSV didn't have a minus sign.
income_categories = ['Salary', 'Refund']

df['Amount'] = df.apply(
    lambda x: abs(x['Amount']) if x['Category'] in income_categories 
    else -abs(x['Amount']), 
    axis=1
)

🎯Strategic Financial Insights (The "Story" in the Data)

  1. The "Income-Expense" Gap (Critical Deficit) Insight: The analysis revealed a Total Outflow of $1.77M against a Total Inflow of $504k, resulting in a -250.3% Savings Rate.

Context: This massive gap suggests that the account holder is likely relying on credit, external loans, or pre-existing high-balance savings. From a data perspective, it shows that the "Burn Rate" is 3.5x higher than the earning rate.

  1. Fixed Cost Dominance: Insight: Insurance is the #1 expense category, accounting for 14.5% of all spending ($256,010).

Observation: Unlike dining or travel, insurance is usually a fixed premium. This indicates that the user's "Non-Negotiable" living costs are extremely high, making it harder to save even if they cut back on lifestyle spending.

  1. Retail Behavior: The "Flipkart" Effect Insight: Flipkart is both the Top Merchant by spend and the Most Frequent destination for transactions.

Context: This highlights a heavy reliance on e-commerce. A significant portion of the budget is leaking through frequent retail purchases rather than organized bills or utilities.

  1. Seasonal Volatility: The October Peak Insight: Spending peaked significantly in October 2025, reaching over $118,000 in a single month.

Recommendation: This suggests "Seasonal Spending" (perhaps holiday shopping or annual insurance renewals). For better financial health, these annual peaks should be spread across the year through better "Sinking Fund" planning.

  1. Category Pareto Principle Insight: Just 3 categories (Insurance, Travel, and Utilities) account for nearly 40% of all expenses.

Actionable Advice: To move the savings rate from -250% toward 0%, the user should focus on negotiating insurance premiums or optimizing travel costs rather than worrying about smaller categories like "Dining," which have a negligible impact on the total deficit.
